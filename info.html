Park History


Brookmere National Park has been established since October 1, 1890. It is 759,620 acres (3,074 km^2) big. It has a long history of habitation, scientific discovery, and protection efforts. Native American tribes vacated the land of Brookmere for thousands of years prior to colonization, and important figures like John Muir fought for the land's preservation. Brookmere welcomes visitors with its natural granite mountains, beautiful waterfalls, and diverse ecosystems. It was recognized as the first protected wildland in the United States, with the Brookmere Grant drafted and signed in 1864. This set the stage for the national park system we have in the US today, with over 150 protected national parks. Today, Brookmere is remembered by its long history of conservation and preservation, inspiring millions of visitors every year with its natural beauty and serving as a reminder that the preservation of Mother Nature pays off.
