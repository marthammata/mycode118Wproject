Our Impact

Preservation



At Brookmere National Park, the community focuses on preservation of the natural habitat. Since 1890, we have been trying to save the Black Bear's habitat, threathened by forest fires and deforestation. We offer free brochures on the topic of preservation and threats to natural habitats at Brookmere for the education of our fellow community.


Habitat Restoration

Our ongoing mission is to preserve and enhance the park's diverse ecosystems. We're dedicated to rehabilitating forest and grass areas, restoring native plant communities, and creating better habitat conditions for wildlife. Through activities like invasive species removal, reforestation, and erosion control, we're working to ensure the recovery of endangered species and the retainment and growth of biodiversity. Guided by scientific research and ecological principles, we collaborate with conservation organizations, park staff, and our community volunteers to implement these restorative activities and projects. Together, we're ensuring the long-term health of Brookmere natural land for generations (of both people and wildlife!) to come. Join us in our restoration journey!


Education

In our state Fourth Grader Education program, all Fourth Graders attending an accredited California public school has free admission to any national park in CA, including Brookmere. We have granted 678,000 free Fourth Grader admissions in this time, allowing education about Brookmere and fellow state parks to flourish. Learn our visiting hours for educational trips here!



Scientific Research

Brookmere hosts many scientific projects and is home to many researchers and rehabilitists. Researchers are dedicated to studying our park's ecosystems, wildlife, geology, and climate. They study and analyze topics like species population trends, habitat health, fire weather patterns, glacial changes, and the impacts of climate change. New innovative technologies and monitoring systems are used to gather data, while analyses derived from collaborations with universities and research institutions create a deeper understanding of Brookmere. The findings from these year-long studies provide valuable insights for park management, conservation strategies, and the preservation of Brookemere's ecological integrity. The research being conducted in Brookemere ensures the park remains a centre for scientific discovery and will benefit generations of ecological systems to come.
